Ten Years of Us

2016 — 2026 · A Story Worth Telling

🏫 8th Standard
Same Class, Different Worlds

8th standard — new classroom, familiar strangers. We sat in the same room, opened the same textbooks, heard the same teachers... and yet barely exchanged a word. Ninety percent silence. Not awkward, not intentional — just two people yet to find a reason to speak. Neither of us knew the other would one day matter so much.

📖 9th Standard
Almost, But Not Quite Yet

9th standard brought new chapters — in textbooks and in us. The silence between us softened just a little. A few words here, a small exchange there. Not quite friends, not complete strangers anymore. We were somewhere in between — two people slowly becoming aware of each other, tiptoeing toward something neither of us had named yet.

🎯 10th Standard
The Year We Actually Talked

10th standard — the most important year academically, and quietly, the most important year between us too. Board exams, pressure, late study sessions... and somewhere in all of it, real conversations finally happened. Not just classroom small talk, but actual talking. The kind where you forget the time. After two years of near-silence, it turned out we had a lot to say to each other.

❤️ 11th Standard
The Day I Got a Sister

11th standard changed everything. The conversations from 10th had quietly grown into something deeper — a bond that needed a name. And somewhere between shared laughs and long talks, I found myself calling you Sister. Not out of obligation, not out of tradition — but because it felt true. That one word said everything: you weren't just a friend anymore. You were family.

🦠 12th Standard
One Text in a Silent World

The world shut down in 2020. Schools closed, streets emptied, and 12th standard — the year that was supposed to be everything — became something unrecognisable. Amid all that silence and uncertainty, we had just one text message between us. One. But somehow, in a year where the whole world felt lost, that one message was enough to say — I still remember you.

🎓 College Begins
Closer Than Ever, Miles Apart

School ended, and a new chapter began — college life, new places, new people. We went our separate ways physically, yet something strange happened: the bond only grew stronger. No shared classroom, no passing in hallways, no seeing each other's face — just words on a screen. And somehow those words built something deeper than three years of sitting in the same room ever did. We were closer than ever, without ever being in the same place.

📱 Still Going Strong
Two Years Deep, No Distance

Second year of college, and the distance between us had stopped feeling like distance at all. We had found our rhythm — a friendship that didn't need proximity to survive. Late night texts, random voice notes, sharing the smallest moments of college life with each other. No one would have guessed we hadn't seen each other in years. Some bonds don't need a room to breathe in.

🌙 Three Years Apart
Unseen, Unforgotten

Three years of college and not once did our paths cross in person. Yet here we were — still talking, still checking in, still a part of each other's daily life. It was the kind of friendship that quietly proved itself every single day without needing to. No meetup, no photograph together, just an unspoken promise that we'd always show up for each other — even from afar.

🤝 Finally, Face to Face
Worth Every Single Wait

2024. After three long years of college life spent talking through screens — we finally saw each other again. No words could quite describe what that felt like. All those texts, all those late night calls, all that time — it had built something so solid that seeing each other in person felt like no time had passed at all. Some reunions don't need to be dramatic. This one just felt like coming home.

🌸 March 7, 2025
The Meeting That Changed Everything

March 7, 2025 — a date worth remembering. We met after a long, long gap, and something about that day hit differently. You made me see things I had been blind to. How you had always been there — quietly, consistently, without asking for anything in return. And somewhere in that realisation came the weight of knowing I hadn't always given you the same. I hurt you. More than once, maybe more than I know. And I am sorry, Sister — truly, deeply sorry. You deserved better every single time. Thank you for staying anyway.

🎂 Today
Ten Years & Counting

And here we are — ten years from that first day in a shared classroom, standing at a moment worth celebrating. Every chapter led to this one. Happy Birthday, Sister. Here's to the years already written, and to all the beautiful pages still ahead. 🎉
